## Track 3: Public Speaking & Communication

**Goal:** Be the person people listen to in meetings. Present with clarity and confidence.

### Weekly Practice
| Day | Activity | Time |
|-----|----------|------|
| Mon/Wed/Fri | Record yourself explaining a concept (2 min) | 15-20 min |
| Tue/Thu | Shadow a great speaker (Andrew Ng, Simon Sinek, Jobs) | 15 min |
| Weekend | Toastmasters meeting (find a club - check SAP internal first) | 1-2 hrs |

### At Work (Free, High Impact)
- [ ] Volunteer to present/demo in every sprint review
- [ ] Ask questions in meetings (even small ones build the muscle)
- [ ] Explain technical decisions to non-technical stakeholders
- [ ] Offer to run knowledge-sharing sessions on GenAI topics you know

### Frameworks to Internalize
- **PREP** (Point, Reason, Example, Point) - for meetings and standups
- **3 Act** (Hook, 3 Points, Close with callback) - for presentations
- **Feynman Technique** (simplify, use analogies) - for technical explanations

### Speakers to Study
| Speaker | Learn What |
|---------|-----------|
| Andrew Ng | Explaining complex things simply |
| Simon Sinek | Opening hooks, storytelling |
| Steve Jobs | Pauses, tension, minimalism |

## Rules

1. **No zero days on DSA.** Even 1 easy problem counts. The streak matters more than intensity.
2. **AI courses on weekends.** Don't let them eat into DSA time on weekdays.
3. **Speaking is daily.** 15 minutes minimum. Non-negotiable.
4. **Weekly review every Sunday.** 30 min. Check this file. Update checkboxes. Be honest.
5. **One thing at a time during each time block.** No multitasking.
